Discussion Example

This selected excerpt shows a discussion prompt connecting medical terminology to authentic communication and popular culture.

Discuss: Medical Terminology a Language of its Own

Instructions

Today’s TV doctors and nurses are even more rigorous: They have to know lots of procedures and terminology — and make the fake look real for the camera. Actors who star in medical dramas like House, ER, and Grey's Anatomy have compared learning the necessary medical vocabulary to learning lines of Shakespeare. They have to understand what they are saying and how to pronounce the words for the audience to understand. Outside of the physical action, they are saying lines in what feels like a foreign language. 

To participate in this discussion: 

  1. Watch this YouTube clip of Grey's Anatomy stars learning medical terminology as they practice their lines:

    Note: Please be advised this video contains clips of simulated trauma response, emergency medical situations, and other medical procedures. 

  2. Now that you have begun learning how to approach learning the language of medical terminology,  consider what advice you would offer these actors for learning the medical terminology in their scripts? What steps should they take to learn how to pronounce, break into word parts, and define medical terms? Do you have any additional tips you can offer?
  3. Post your responses to the discussion board linked below. 
  4. Engage with your colleagues by responding to at least two of their posts.
