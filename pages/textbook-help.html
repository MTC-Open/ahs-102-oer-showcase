How to Use Your Medical Terminology Textbook

As you navigate through this course, you will find that there are various methods and formats for utilizing the OER Textbook. The unique structure of this course allows you to use the textbook in both PDF and Web-based formats. Both formats have their advantages and differences. Check out these instructions for more information and be sure to review the tips and video walkthrough below.

Tips for Getting the Most out of the Textbook Used in this Course

  • Preview the chapter before you read. Read the title, introduction, and headings, and glance at charts and pictures. A two-to-five-minute preview reduces the total time you spend reading and increases your comprehension. 
  • Turn headings into questions before reading. This will give you a specific purpose for your reading, resulting in better comprehension. Try to begin your questions with "What," "How," and "Why," because they lead to more detailed responses. For example, the heading “Membranes and Layers of the Heart ” can be turned into the question, “What are the membranes and layers of the heart?” By reading to answer the question, your focus will be greatly improved. 
  • Monitor your comprehension. Stop to check your understanding of the material at regular intervals. Stop and reflect on the answers to the Concept Checks as they are presented throughout the text. 
  • Utilize multimedia features. Stop and watch the videos and engage with the interactive activities as they are presented. They can help reinforce your understanding and make learning more enjoyable. Alternating between reading, watching videos, and engaging in interactive exercises can also help you maintain focus and prevent fatigue. 
  • Utilize the study guides provided and take notes as you read the accompanying text. This way, you can have a consolidated and customized reference for your study sessions. 
  • Utilize search and keyword functions. Search within the text to quickly find specific topics or keywords. This can save you time when you need to locate specific information or refer back to previously covered material. Use Ctrl+F (Cmd+F on Mac) to search for key terms in the text. 
  • Mark the text or take notes as you read. Highlight, underline, or take notes as you finish each section. Wait until you finish reading the paragraph or small section before you begin to mark your text. That way you know you are marking up the most important points. There are many free apps available across different devices, including mobile, that allow you to mark up, highlight, and add notes to the text.
  • Practice active reading. Treat the text as an interactive resource. Instead of passively reading through the material, actively engage with it. Ask questions, make connections to previous knowledge, and try to explain concepts in your own words. This active reading approach enhances comprehension and retention.
  • Develop a study plan that outlines your goals and the topics you need to cover. Break down your reading into manageable sections and allocate specific time slots for studying. Stick to your plan to ensure steady progress.
