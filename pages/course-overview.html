Course Overview

This selected excerpt shows how students are oriented to the course structure, expectations, and embedded OER materials.

Overview

Please make sure to read everything in this Course Overview, as well as all other information in the Getting Started module. This information will provide you with an introduction to the course.

Course Structure

This course is organized by modules. In this particular module, you will discover how to contact your instructor, important information to help you through the course, and your required first-week activities. Please be aware that your completion of these required first-week activities will be used to verify your enrollment & activity in this course. If you are not already familiar with working in the D2L learning environment, the Before you Begin module will be especially helpful for you.  In addition to technical information, this module provides you with important information regarding how and where to get any assistance you might need throughout the course.

Each module of this course begins with an Overview. You should carefully review the overview document before you begin work on the module content. The overview will provide you with important insight into the material being covered as well as the learning objectives, tasks, and estimated time to complete the module. The learning objectives will enable you to meet the overall course objectives and competencies that will assist you in becoming successful in this course.

Course Materials

Required

This course utilizes free Open Educational Resources (OER). The textbook used for the course is embedded throughout the Content of the course as interactive web pages as well as PDF. Each chapter of the text is embedded directly in the content of each module or you can access the full versions in the Embedded Course Textbook module. 

A Student Companion Study Guide is also provided to accompany each chapter of the text. The resource is broken up by chapter and is embedded directly in the corresponding module. You may also access the Full Student Companion Study Guide in the Embedded Course Textbook module if you would prefer to download or print the resource in full. 

For more information including instructions for how to get the most out of the text used in this course, please review How to Use Your Medical Terminology Textbook.

Course Expectations

Due Dates & Deadlines

This is not a self-paced course. There may be some flexibility when you can work on assignments for your course but keep in mind that all readings, activities, and assessments have associated due dates. To keep track of these dates, you are provided with a Schedule of Activities.

The Schedule of Activities lists all of your activities and assignments throughout the course.  Remember that due dates can be changed at the discretion of your instructor so refer to this document and the course calendar often.

 Online (Asynchronous) Sections: All coursework is due on Wednesday and Friday evenings by 8 pm on the established dates that are listed in the Schedule of Activities.

On-Campus Sections: All coursework is due by or before the beginning of our normally scheduled class times on the established dates that are listed in the Schedule of Activities.

POLICY FOR ACCEPTING LATE WORK: It is very important in any class for you to keep up with due dates on assignments and quizzes. Since this course is preparing you for a career in the healthcare field; your accountability, responsibility & timeliness are extremely important. Because of those factors; late work is not accepted in this course with only the rare exception of cases of documented extenuating circumstances. 

Attendance

Online (Asynchronous) Sections: Your attendance will be taken based on course work completed for the week. You can view your attendance at any time in the Attendance tool.

“P” - If you submit all of your course work by the given due date, you are counted as present
“T” - If you submit only a portion of the course work by the given due date, you are counted as tardy
“A” - If you do not submit any of the course work by the given due date, you are counted as absent

On-Campus Sections: Your attendance will be taken based on your showing up to class in-person, on time and participating in class. You can view your attendance at any time in the Attendance tool.

“P” - If you show up to class by or before the established twice-weekly class start time. 
“T” - If you show up to class after the established twice-weekly class start time.
“A” - If you do not show up to class at all during the established twice-weekly class start time.

Proctored Exam/Assignment

Asynchronous Online and Web-Hybrid sections of this course will be required to take all exams using Respondus Monitor and Lockdown Browser.  Please review the Instructions for Installation and Use page for more information.

Communication Policies

In terms of communication, I prefer to use email. My apologies, but I just don't feel comfortable giving out my personal phone number. And since I do not have my own office (or my own office phone); it's just best to get in touch with me via email. I promise that I will always do my best to respond as timely & as helpfully as possible. As long as I'm not already teaching or working or predisposed with some important task when you email me; I generally respond rather quickly (within a few hours or less) and it's rare that you would ever have to wait more than 24 hours to ever hear from me (or possibly 48 hours over a weekend or during a holiday). Please keep in mind that I do teach other on-campus/online/virtual courses and work elsewhere during the week & sometimes on weekends. So, I view sleep as something that is sacred and I generally turn my phone off around midnight. If you email me sometime after that, then I will promise that I will get back to you ASAP in the morning or at my next earliest convenience. As a reminder, all emails should include your first & last name and course name & section number (e.g. AHS 102 A01). If this information is missing, then you may not receive a prompt response. Communication is a vital key in healthcare and even more so in education; so let's do everything in our power to make sure communication between you & I goes smoothly this semester.
