Unit 1 Overview

This selected excerpt shows how the unit introduces learning objectives, tasks, and time expectations.

Overview

As we enter into the first unit of this course; it's important to understand that medical terminology is not simply the memorization of word parts and definitions. Just as various countries and cultures around the world have their own languages; the world of health care has its own language as well. Learning various word parts and their definitions; as well as how to spell and pronounce full medical terms is how we communicate in the world of health care. We will learn this new language while applying the word parts, definitions and medical terms to the various structures and systems of human anatomy and physiology. This allows us to not only communicate with one another, but to also become better health care providers in the long term. And that is the essence of medical terminology.

In this first module of true course content, we will discuss how Medical Terminology is the "Language of Health Care." While the later chapters in this course are dedicated to specific systems of the body; these first two chapters are about establishing a baseline or a foundation of knowledge that we can build upon throughout the rest of the semester. We will review the basics of Medical Terminology. We will learn common word parts and their definitions. We will practice the rules for combining these word parts and then assess our ability to recognize, define, and spell common terms used in Medical Terminology. 

Next we will apply what we learned about the Language of Medical Terminology to basic human anatomy and physiology. We will begin to apply those various word parts, definitions, and spelling rules to a the human body. We will not only learn what specific parts of the body are being referenced, but also how those specific parts relate to the entire body as a whole. The future modules or chapters in this course will be dedicated to specific systems of the human body, but first we will take a wider view of the entire human body. 

Learning Objectives

After completing this module, you will be able to:

  • Identify the four types of word parts & their roles in forming medical terms
  • Define the most commonly used prefixes, word roots, combining forms, and suffixes that are introduced in this chapter 
  • Begin to analyze unfamiliar medical terms using your knowledge of word parts
  • Recognize, define, spell, and pronounce the most common medical terms
  • Define anatomy & physiology
  • Use anatomic reference terms to identify the anatomic position, body planes, regions, and cavities 
  • Identify the systems of the body and the major structures & functions of each
  • Recognize, define, spell, and pronounce the terms related to the structure, function, pathology, and procedures of cells, tissues, and gland

Task List

  • Complete the Identifying Word Parts in Medical Terms assigned readings, watch the embedded videos, and engage with the interactive practice activities
  • Complete the Knowledge Check Quiz
  • Complete the Apply activities:
    • Word Surgery Identifying Word Parts in Medical Terms 
    • Med Speak Practice 
  • Complete the Medical Terminology Related to Basic Human Anatomy & Physiology assigned readings, watch the embedded videos, and engage with the interactive practice activities
  • Complete the Knowledge Check Quiz
  • Complete the Apply activities:
    • Word Surgery Medical Language Related to the Body
    • Med Speak Medical Language Related to the Body
  • Complete the Anatomy Lab
  • Participate in the Medical Terminology a Language of its Own discussion
  • Complete the Unit 1 Exam. 

Estimated Time to Complete this Module

This unit will take approximately 10-15  hours to complete.
